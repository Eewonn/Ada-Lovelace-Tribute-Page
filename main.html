Who Was Ada Lovelace?

Contribution

It wasn't until the 1950s that Lovelace's contributions to computer science were recognized. B.V. Bowden reintroduced her notes to the world. Faster Than Thought: A Symposium on Digital Computing Machines, written by Bowden, was reissued in 1953. Ada has now won numerous posthumous awards for her efforts. 1980 saw the U.S. A recently created computer language was given the name "Ada," in honor of Lovelace, by the Department of Defense.
